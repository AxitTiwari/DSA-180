**Dear Future Me,**

I am so, so glad to tell you that the journey we promised ourselves on **14th January** has finally reached its end.

Today, I completed **180 days of DSA**, just like we promised.

I'm sharing a few photos below—not to prove anything to anyone, but so that whenever you come back here years later, you'll remember where all of this started.

If someone looks at these pictures, they'll probably notice playlists, solved problems, GitHub commits and progress.

But you and I know... this journey was never about those numbers.

It started with around **80 days of the Love Babbar playlist**, where I built my foundation. Then came nearly **60 days of solving problems on my own**, making mistakes, debugging, reading editorials, and learning how to think instead of just following solutions. After that, I completed the **NeetCode 150**, and in these last few weeks, I focused on understanding DSA patterns rather than individual questions.

Looking back, I don't think DSA is the biggest thing I gained.

The biggest thing was proving to myself that I can stay committed to something for months.

And what makes this even more special is that it's just a coincidence that **tomorrow my college starts again.** It almost feels like one chapter is quietly ending while another one is waiting for me to begin.

Now, I want to say a few things to you.

I know I was late.

Actually... **very late.**

And I'm sorry for that.

Maybe if I had started earlier, things would have looked different today.

But maybe this was the maximum that I was capable of giving at this stage of life. And if that's true, then I'm proud that I gave it honestly.

I know I wasn't perfectly consistent.

I missed days.

I got distracted.

Sometimes I wasted time that I can never get back.

But I also know that I kept coming back.

And maybe that's what mattered the most.

I know you're emotional.

Probably more than you should be.

Please don't let every opinion, every rejection, every comparison, or every expectation steal your peace.

Feel things, but don't let them stop you.

And yes... I know distractions are still your biggest enemy.

If you're reading this in the future, I hope you've learned to control them better than I ever could.

Because the person you want to become deserves your full attention.

I don't know where life has taken you while you're reading this.

Maybe you've achieved things that today feel impossible.

Maybe you're still struggling.

Either way, I hope you've become a little kinder, a little stronger, and a little wiser than the person writing this today.

This isn't the end of learning DSA.

It's simply the end of a promise made on **14th January.**

From tomorrow, college starts again.

A new semester.

New challenges.

A new version of life.

I wish you all the very best for this new chapter.

Take care of yourself.

Keep building.

And there's just one thing I want from you.

**If we ever get the chance to meet...**

**I hope I don't recognize you.**

Not because I want you to become someone else, but because I hope you've grown so much that the person writing this letter feels like a complete stranger.

I hope your dreams become bigger than mine.

I hope your discipline becomes stronger than mine.

I hope your heart becomes calmer than mine.

And if one day we really do meet...

I hope I look at you with a smile and quietly say,

*"Thank you for not giving up on us."*

I'll be waiting to meet you someday.

— **Axit**
